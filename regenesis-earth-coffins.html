Frequently Asked Questions

Understanding our regenerative approach

Is it safe for groundwater? +

Yes. Our coffins use only natural, plant-based materials with no toxic chemicals or treatments. The decomposition process is completely natural and poses no risk to groundwater or surrounding ecosystems. We've worked with environmental scientists to ensure the highest safety standards.

How long does it take to decompose? +

Depending on soil conditions, moisture, and climate, our biodegradable coffins fully decompose within 3-5 years. This natural timeline allows for gradual nutrient release into the soil, optimizing the regenerative benefits for the land.

Is it culturally respectful? +

Absolutely. ReGenesis was developed in close consultation with cultural leaders, community elders, and spiritual advisors across multiple African communities. Our design honors traditional burial practices while aligning with the timeless principle of returning to the earth.

Is it fully biodegradable? +

Yes. Every component is made from 100% natural, biodegradable materials including sustainably harvested plant fibers, natural adhesives, and organic dyes. There are no plastics, metals, or synthetic materials used in our coffins.

Can a tree be planted above it? +

Yes! Planting a tree is highly encouraged. As the coffin decomposes, it enriches the soil with nutrients that nourish the tree's growth. This creates a living memorial that honors your loved one while contributing to reforestation and ecological restoration.

What makes this different from traditional coffins? +

Traditional coffins often use treated wood, metals, and synthetic materials that don't break down naturally. ReGenesis coffins are designed to become part of the earth, actively contributing to soil health rather than remaining as inert objects underground.
